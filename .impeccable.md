## Design Context

### Users
Potential clients looking to book guided fly fishing trips on the Missouri River near Craig, Montana. They range from first-timers who've never held a fly rod to experienced anglers seeking local expertise on specific water. Their context: researching outfitters online, comparing options, deciding whether to trust this crew with their time and money. Many are out-of-state visitors; some are military/first responders eligible for the Heroes rate. The job to be done: find a credible, local outfitter and book a trip with confidence.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Adventurous, Local, Honest

**Voice:** Direct and conversational. First-person where it fits. Short sentences. No marketing fluff, no superlatives, no stock phrases. The copy sounds like a guide talking to you at the boat ramp — knowledgeable but not showing off. "No pretense" is the brand's own tagline and the governing principle of all communication.

**Emotional goal:** Trust and calm confidence. The visitor should feel they're in good hands — competent, reliable, low-pressure. The site earns credibility through specificity (license numbers, named guides, real bios) rather than polish.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone:** Earthy, restrained, grounded. Dark river tones (deep teal-greens) anchor the palette, with warm bone/ivory for contrast. The design should feel like late-afternoon light on the Missouri — warm but not bright, natural but not rustic-kitschy.

**Design system:** "Teal & Bone" (DS-002) — full spec in `docs/lomo-ui-guide.md`
- **Colors:** `river-deep` (#1A2E30), `river-mid` (#2A4A4E), `teal` (#3E9E98), `teal-dark` (#2C7A75), `bone` (#F4F1EB), `stone` (#7A8E8F)
- **Typography:** Playfair Display (display/headlines — authoritative serif), Source Sans 3 (body — clean, readable sans)
- **Named font scale:** Use `text-eyebrow`, `text-h1`, `text-h2`, `text-stat`, `text-price`, etc. — never arbitrary rem values
- **Spacing:** `py-section` (3.5rem) for all section padding, `max-w-site` (1280px) container
- **Corners:** `rounded-card` (3px), `rounded-btn` (2px) — sharp, not soft
- **Section wrapper:** `<section class="w-full py-section"><div class="max-w-site mx-auto px-6">...</div></section>`

**Teal discipline:** Teal appears in exactly one role per section — either as a CTA button or as accent text (stat numbers, eyebrow labels), never both in the same section.

**Anti-references:**
- NOT slick/corporate outdoor brands — no over-produced lifestyle imagery, no "adventure awaits" marketing speak, no feeling of a big brand
- NOT cheap/cluttered booking platforms — no overwhelming options, no stock photos, no generic templates
- Stay in the authentic middle: professional enough to trust, personal enough to feel real

**Theme:** Light sections alternate with dark (`bone`/`white` vs `river-deep`/`river-mid`), creating a natural rhythm. No dark-mode toggle — the dark sections ARE the dark mode.

### Design Principles

1. **Specificity over polish.** License numbers, named guides, real bios, concrete prices. Every detail that proves this is a real operation earns more trust than any amount of visual refinement.

2. **Restraint is the aesthetic.** Tight color palette, minimal border radii, generous whitespace, no decorative elements. The river photos and typography do the work — everything else stays out of the way.

3. **One accent per section.** Teal is the only accent color. It appears once per section in a single role. This discipline prevents visual noise and keeps the eye moving to the right place.

4. **Copy is UI.** The voice ("No pretense") is as much a design element as the color palette. Never add placeholder copy, lorem ipsum, or generic microcopy. If real copy isn't available, leave a visible `[PLACEHOLDER]` marker.

5. **Mobile-first, scroll-friendly.** Single-column stacks on mobile, no horizontal scrolling, tap-to-call on all phone numbers, generous touch targets. The site should work as well from a truck in Craig as from a desktop in Denver.

### Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- Semantic HTML throughout (proper heading hierarchy, landmark regions, form labels)
- All images require descriptive alt text
- Keyboard navigable — no mouse-only interactions
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for any animations
- Color contrast ratios meet AA minimums (verify teal-on-dark and stone-on-bone combinations)
