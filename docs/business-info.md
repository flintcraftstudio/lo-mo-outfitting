## Guided Rivers

Lo Mo Outfitting offers guided fly fishing trips on four premier Montana rivers: the Missouri, Bitterroot, Madison, and Yellowstone.

### Missouri River

The Missouri River outside of Craig, Montana offers some of the best fly fishing in the world. Fed by the confluence of the Jefferson, Madison, and Gallatin Rivers, this Blue Ribbon trout river holds an estimated 7,000 fish per mile. Guests on guided trips can expect fish ranging from 16–21 inches. Hatch activity includes midges, blue wing olives, pale morning duns, tricos, caddis, and hoppers later in the summer — making for excellent dry fly fishing throughout the season.

### Bitterroot River

A tailwater fishery below Painted Rock Reservoir near Darby, Montana, the Bitterroot offers some of the best dry fly fishing in the state. The West Fork of the river sees skwala hatches in March and April, followed by blue winged olives, march browns, and caddis as spring progresses — bringing cutthroat trout to the surface. Summer hatches include salmon flies, golden stones, drakes, PMDs, and caddis.

### Madison River

The lower section of the Madison River, below Ennis Lake Dam, offers exceptional year-round fishing for rainbow trout, Westslope cutthroat, and brown trout. Spring hatches bring midges, blue wing olives, march browns, and the famous Mother's Day caddis hatch. Summer transitions to pale morning duns, salmon flies, yellow sallies, golden stoneflies, more caddis, and tricos. Late summer shifts toward terrestrial patterns — hoppers, ants, and beetles.

## Our Story

Lo Mo Outfitting LLC was founded in 2024 by a Montana-born outdoorsman with a lifelong passion for fly fishing. With 20+ years of experience on the Missouri River and time spent fishing rivers across the state, guiding was a natural calling.

Matt began his guiding career as a hunting guide in his early twenties before transitioning to part-time fly fishing guide work on the Missouri — all while working 50 hours a week in concrete manufacturing. In his second year of guiding, he made the leap to pursue fly fishing full time. Over the years, he earned his outfitter's license and founded Lo Mo Outfitting to share Montana's world-class rivers with anglers from all walks of life.
